Verbs


Convention/Grammar

Verbs are important words that tell us what actions are happening. They help us understand what someone is doing, thinking, or feeling.

Here are some key points about verbs:

Verbs Show Actions: Verbs are action words. For example, in the sentence “The dog runs,” the word “runs” is the verb because it shows what the dog is doing.

Verbs Describe States: Verbs can also describe a state of being or feeling. For instance, the verb “think” describes what someone is doing mentally. In the sentence “She thinks about her homework,” the word “thinks” is the verb because it tells us what she is doing in her mind.

Every Sentence Needs a Verb: Every complete sentence has at least one verb because it tells us what’s happening. For example, “They play soccer.” Here, “play” is the verb showing the action.
